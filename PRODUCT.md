# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Independent and retail algo-traders, quantitative investors, and capital-markets builders — the same r/algotrading, r/quant, and QuantConnect crowd the pipeline draws its sources from. They open this in the morning to stay current, usually on their own time and their own machine rather than at a trading desk. The job to be done is simple to state and hard to do by hand: keep up with how Applied AI, agentic AI, and machine learning are changing investment research, backtesting, execution, portfolio construction, risk, and analysis of filings, earnings, and news — without spending an hour a day wading through repos, forums, and papers to find the few things that matter.

Success looks like this becoming the trusted daily filter for that audience: a public product and newsletter people come back to each morning because it reliably surfaces the signal they'd otherwise miss.

## Product Purpose

Trading Alpha Engine ingests updates from the projects, communities, and builders working on AI/ML across capital markets: trading and execution, investment research, portfolio construction, risk, financial NLP, and time-series forecasting. It routes them through an LLM that scores relevance and writes a short technical summary plus a practitioner-impact note, then presents the survivors as a filterable insight feed and a daily newsletter. It exists because the raw stream is overwhelming and mostly noise; the product's whole reason to exist is to do the filtering and framing so its readers don't have to. It ships as a public product for the capital-markets builder community, not an internal tool or a demo.

## Positioning

Every update is read and scored by an LLM, so you only read the alpha, not the noise. Relevance is the product: a reproducible technique, tool, model, dataset, or result for capital markets earns attention; a market take or macro commentary does not. The value is not that it collects everything, but that it decides what is worth your attention and tells you why.

## Brand Personality

Calm, editorial, and curated. The voice is measured, selective, and literate — it reads like a well-kept daily brief written by someone with taste, not a feed firehose or a hype channel. It carries real expertise but wears it lightly: confident and plain-spoken, never breathless, never intimidating. Restraint is a feature; the product earns trust by being consistently worth the two minutes it asks for.

## Anti-references

Do not look or feel like generic AI-generated SaaS — the cream-tinted background, gradient accents, glassmorphic cards, and big-number hero-metric templates that read as "an AI made this." That aesthetic is the opposite of the credibility this product needs; distinctiveness and restraint are the point, and any drift back toward that default is a regression.

## Design Principles

Signal over volume. Every screen should make relevance legible and push noise out of view; the score, the category, and the impact note are the product, and the interface should keep them front and center rather than burying them in chrome.

Curated, not comprehensive. Show the best, not everything. Completeness is the pipeline's job; the reader's experience is about taste and editing. When in doubt, cut.

Approachable expertise. The material is serious and technical, but a self-taught retail builder should never feel out of their depth. Deliver depth through calm, well-structured reading, not jargon walls or intimidation.

Earn trust through transparency. Say why something matters (the practitioner-impact note), cite the source, and never overstate. For a public product asking to be someone's daily filter, credibility is the whole game.

A calm daily ritual. Respect the morning-read context: unhurried, scannable, and quiet. The product should feel like a good habit, never like another noisy feed competing for attention.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA: sufficient contrast, full keyboard access, and properly labeled controls throughout. The current build meets this bar and it should stay met as the product grows.
